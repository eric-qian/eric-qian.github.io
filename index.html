About

Favorite coffee recipe: This pourover coffee recipe yields half of a liter of coffee. I typically prepare this with beans from Counter Culture Coffee and a Hario V60 dripper.

  1. Preparation: Begin by boiling one liter of water in a gooseneck kettle. Weigh 46 grams of freshly roasted coffee beans and grind to medium-fine coarseness. Place paper filter inside coffee dripper and pre-wet with boiling water. Dispose water. Evenly distribute freshly ground coffee into dripper before making a slight divot in center.
  2. Prewetting: Start timer and quickly pour 92 grams of water into dripper. Gently shake dripper to ensure even water distribution. At the 40 second mark, quickly pour water over grounds in concentric circles until the dripper is full.
  3. Pour: Next, in concentric circles, slowly pour water into dripper, making sure to hit dark spots. Periodically shake the dripper to ensure that the bed of coffee grounds is even.
  4. Evaluation: Stop pouring after pouring a total of 540 grams of water. Stop timer, aiming for an elapsed time of 3-4 minutes. If the elapsed time is greater than 4 minutes, use coarser grounds. If the elapsed time is less than 3 minutes, use finer grounds.
